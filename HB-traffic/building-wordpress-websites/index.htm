Choosing A Web Hosting Provider

When I talk about building a website, I like to describe it as building a house, that is, a digital address on the internet that you and your business can be located. To build a house, you’ll need a piece of land to build on. In building WordPress websites, you need web hosting.

What is Web Hosting?

Well, Web hosting is a service that allows you to store the data and files that make up your websites on a server. It’s like a garage service that allows you to park in your car for a specific period, for a fee, only this time, It’s your website. It’s like renting a space. More like a digital warehouse to safely store up the files and data of your website.

3 Hosting packages you might want to consider for starters.

There are different web hosting packages that you can choose from to host your website. These packages come with different offers that are tailored to meet your needs and budget. So, you get to choose a hosting package based on the website you want to build and your budget.

Choosing a web hosting package can sometimes be a headache even for the big boys, but here are 3 options for you to choose from to build websites using WordPress:

Shared Hosting
Virtual Private Server (VPS)
Managed WordPress Hosting

Shared Hosting

Many Web hosting providers offer the option of shared hosting that allows hundreds of websites to be hosted on a single server, thus making it very affordable. Because this option allows hundreds of websites to be housed on a single server, the cost of hosting is therefore shared by the users. For this reason, you can get a shared hosting package for a low as $5.

It’s like having a next next-door neighbour with who you get to share the general cost of building maintenance. It is the perfect option if you are on a very tight budget, but it sure comes with its disadvantages.

Because there are over hundreds of websites hosted on a single server, there is usually the problem of security and poor uptime when you build websites using WordPress on Shared Hosting. An attack on one website can easily affect every other user on the server without any warning. Your website can technically go down at any time due to the limited available memory and unpredictable events.

A single user can take up to 70% of the memory space, leaving hundreds of other users to scramble for what’s left. I mean, you can imagine your website going down just while a customer or a potential client is checking you out. You definitely wouldn’t want that to happen, would you?

Virtual Private Servers (VPS)

This is just like the shared hosting package, but a bit better. The VPS hosting package option allows multiple users on a single server, but this time, the hardware is evenly partitioned among a fewer number of users on the same server.

It’s a better version of the shared hosting package with most, if not all, of its downsides taken away. It gives you the chance to do more with your own space on the server, reduces the risk of attacks from other users, and gives better server administration options.

Unfortunately, you get to pay up to 3 times the amount you would for a shared hosting option. This is worth it, considering the better security, reduced uncertainty, and super flexibility.

Managed WordPress Hosting

Here comes the easy deal, the managed WordPress hosting. This option works perfectly for building WordPress websites. It’s like getting the whole server from WordPress, thus every bit of the website is structured to work with WordPress. This option offers better security and makes your websites a lot faster than when hosted using the other options. With managed WordPress hosting, building websites using WordPress becomes even easier.

Since all skeletal structure of your website is configured to best work with WordPress, you might have a tiny bit of trouble installing other systems and plugins not approved by the host. Simply put, it reduces your flexibility, but ensure your website functions at its best based on what the host, considers to be efficient.

I usually would recommend this option for users who do not have the knowledge, skill and time for sophisticated server settings and installations.

Selecting And Installing A Theme To Design your Website

You are almost there now. Let’s talk about designing your website using a theme that supports the kind of website you want to create. There are thousands of different themes to select from in building WordPress websites, all tailored to meet specific niche and user needs.

A theme is quite necessary for Building WordPress Websites

WordPress themes are templates designed by professionals to give your website its visual appearance and can be used to change your website appearance for the best user experience depending on your niche or the kind of website you are building.

Every WordPress website comes with a default theme that looks just like the image below but you don’t have to worry. You can change that at any point.

To change your WordPress theme, you will need to select and install a theme that works best for your website.

First, visit the admin dashboard of WordPress, then click on the Appearance button. This should bring you to the themes page by default, then click on add new.

You will see thousands of both free and paid WordPress themes to select from. You can use the preview button to test each theme to see how it suits your website and if you like it. If you are satisfied with what you see, just click on the Install button and wait for a few seconds for your theme to be installed.

Great! You just installed a theme. That was very easy, wasn’t it? You can always do one or two changes to your theme by using the customize option at any time on our website. You see, to build websites using WordPress can be a piece of cake if you know what to do.

Installing Necessary Plugins

Plugins are special apps that brings special features and functionality when installed on your WordPress website. Just as mobile apps are to your smartphones, so are plugins to your WordPress website. They vary based on features and functionality. When learning how to build WordPress websites, plugins are essential.

Some plugins are free and can be installed without paying or purchasing premium access, while others are not. Also, some are installed on every WordPress website by default and some would have to be installed by the site admin.

Plugins are quite versatile in function and their functions can vary from comment management to security, to social media, to search engine optimization, to forms, to galleries, etc. Some of the important plugins you might want to install when you build websites using WordPress are as follows:

Yoast SEO – takes care of your search engine optimization functions, preparing your sites for search engines in general.
Wordfence Security – You would need this to secure your WordPress site.
WPForms – with WPForm, your readers can reach you directly, leaving you with their contact details, and message in particular.
UpdraftPlus – This is perfect for automatic backups of your website.
Google Analytics for WordPress – bridges your websites with the best of Google’s analytic apps and services including the Adsense for displaying ads on your WordPress website.

To build websites using WordPress, you should know how to install WordPress plugins

Installing A Plugin On While Building WordPress Website

With WordPress, installing a plugin is a piece of cake. With just a few clicks, you can get the best plugins needed for a great user experience on your website. Allow me to help you install your first plugin with these few steps:

Click on your WordPress admin dashboard and then click on Plugins to view the plugins already installed on your website.

Click on add new to view the WordPress database of plugins.

Type in the name of your plugins or the functionality you are looking for, and then hit the search button.

Click on install to install the plugin and once done, click on activate to get it running on your website.

That’s it! You just installed a plugin on your WordPress website. You can also at any moment deactivate or uninstall a plugin if you feel it’s affecting the speed of your website or you find it less satisfactory and want to change to another plugin.

All you need to do is return to your plugin page from your WordPress admin dashboard, locate the plugin you which to remove, then click on deactivate or uninstall

Setting Up Important web pages For Building WordPress Website

Another necessity in building WordPress websites are web pages. Every website, irrespective of what kind of website they are, must have certain web pages that present basic information and details for readers and visitors. Now that you have installed your themes and plugins, you should have the following web pages running on your WordPress website.

Home Page – The home page is where your readers will land when they type in your domain name on a browser or just click on your link. It’s the central location of your websites. It’s like the sitting room where you welcome readers and visitors to your website, so it must carry a bit of everything necessary to give a great first impression to your reader.

About page – This page is where your readers go to find out all they need to know about you, your business, or your website. It contains details of your product and services, goals and objectives, team and members, services partners, and departments.

Contact Page – Just as the name implies, this is where your visitors go to get your contact details or leave a message for you. If you have WPForms installed on your WordPress website, your contact page should show a form that allows visitors to leave their contact details with you through an interactive form. This is very necessary when you build websites using WordPress.

How To Setup web-pages On WordPress

Go to your WordPress admin dashboard and click on Pages, and the Add new to display the image below and edit and your contents accordingly.

(1) Title – Here, you should type in the title for the page. E.g About
(2) The body section – You should type in the main content of the page here. You can copy and paste your text from another document or just feel free to type your text.
(3) Add Image – You can add images by clicking this button.
(4) Text or Visual – Switch between the Text and Visual editors. Since you are not quite conversant with HTML codes, you should leave this on visual while you add your text to the body of the page.
(5) Publish Section – The Publish section is where you make your page go live when you are done.
(6) Discussion – You can make settings for comments here. Make sure you have it ticked if you wish to receive comments on your web pages. I will advise you don’t allow comments if you don’t have time to manage spam, a lot of them. The “trackbacks and ping-backs” setting you can leave unchecked too.
(7) Featured image – For this, you might not need to worry. Most WordPress themes have default featured images and display for certain pages, but you can change them to your taste from here
